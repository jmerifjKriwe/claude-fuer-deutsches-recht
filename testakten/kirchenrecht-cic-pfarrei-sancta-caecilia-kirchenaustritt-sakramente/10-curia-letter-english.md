# English Canon Law Note — for the Anglican fiancé and St Botolph's Parish

**From:** Parish Office Sancta Caecilia, Dr Burkhard Tessenow, Parish Priest
**To:** The Revd Canon Hilary Marchbanks, St Botolph's, and Mr Jasper Ellingworth
**Re: Forthcoming marriage of Mr Jasper Ellingworth and Ms Kunigunde von Rabenfels — canonical requirements on the Catholic side**

Dear Canon Marchbanks, dear Mr Ellingworth,

Thank you for your kind enquiry. Allow me to set out, in plain terms, what Catholic canon law requires of us for the forthcoming wedding, and what it does **not** require of Mr Ellingworth.

## 1. Two errors we are trying to avoid

The parish seeks to avoid two opposite mistakes: treating a civil withdrawal from the Church (a German peculiarity concerning Ms von Rabenfels' mother) as if baptism had disappeared — it cannot disappear (canon 849) — and treating the same withdrawal as if it had no ecclesial significance at all. Neither error affects the bride: Kunigunde remains a Catholic in good standing.

## 2. The marriage itself: a "mixed marriage", and what that means

Since Mr Ellingworth was baptised in the Church of England — a baptism we recognise without re-baptism — the union is a **mixed marriage** under canon 1124, not a case of "disparity of cult" (canon 1086, which concerns the unbaptised). The practical consequences:

- The parish needs a **permission (licentia)** from the diocesan bishop's office, not a dispensation. This is routine.
- Under canon 1125, **only the Catholic party makes promises**: Kunigunde declares that she will remain a Catholic and will do all in her power to have the children baptised and raised as Catholics. **Mr Ellingworth signs nothing and promises nothing**; he is simply to be informed of her promises in good time, which this letter also serves to document. His conscience is not bound by our law.
- Both parties are instructed on the essential properties of marriage — unity and indissolubility (canons 1055, 1056) — which Anglican doctrine shares.

## 3. Form of the ceremony

The wedding will take place at Sancta Caecilia before the parish priest and two witnesses (canonical form, canon 1108). We warmly invite Canon Marchbanks to take part — readings, exhortation and blessing are very welcome; a joint celebration of the Eucharist is not possible, and we would in any case recommend a Liturgy of the Word, so that neither congregation is placed in an awkward position regarding Holy Communion (canon 844 § 4 makes eucharistic sharing an exception, not the rule).

## 4. One point we must clarify first

We understand Mr Ellingworth was civilly married from 2014 to 2017. Because Anglicans are not bound by the Catholic canonical form, that earlier marriage may well be **valid in our law** if his former wife was baptised — in which case the impediment of the bond (canon 1085) would bar a new marriage until a Church tribunal has examined the first one. May we kindly ask for: the 2014 marriage certificate, the 2017 decree absolute, and any information on the former wife's baptism. We cannot fix a wedding date before this is resolved; please treat this as the most urgent item.

## 5. Documents requested from St Botolph's

- Certificate of Mr Ellingworth's baptism (certified copy);
- any record concerning the 2014 marriage held in your registers.

With every good wish, and in the hope of a celebration that honours both our traditions,

Dr Burkhard Tessenow
Parish Priest, Sancta Caecilia
